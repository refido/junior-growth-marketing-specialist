Sales inflicts a ton of wounds onto Marketing.

This is why the CMO is constantly being scrutinized.

And most don't realize that its being done to them.

Whenever we work with CMOs to build out a Demand Acquisition System for driving inbound leads that fill their pipeline, here are the five things we do to protect Marketing:

1. Own the entire Lead to Meeting Booked experience

We always install a Microsite when we are building out the Demand Acquisition System. This means that every inbound lead gets taken to a controlled activation experience where we build trust with the brand and drive to book Sales Meetings. 

Why? a) Because we want to be measured by Meetings Booked. and b) 99 things go wrong if you rely on Sales to book the meeting with an inbound lead. No more SQLs, MQLs, or any of that. Just Activated Leads. That's it.

2. Own the BDR Function

With our main metric being Booked Meetings, everything else flows from there. We also own the BDR function. Our people, inside of Marketing, follows up on Leads, nurtures leads, and drives them to the Microsite to activate and book meetings (or does it for them). 

Why? a) Because Sales Reps are the worst at following up with leads that aren't active deals yet *BUT* they'll pay attention to booked meetings on their calendar. b) In today's AI world, a real human touch goes a long way for prospects to trust a brand. c) Every response with a question is feedback to us in Marketing to address objections and confusion in the Marketing journey.

3. We do a Sales and Marketing Pipeline Review every Week

Every week, we sit with sales, and we review the meetings getting booked, the sales process, and trace exactly how it flows through the Sales Process and Playbook in place. 

Why? a) Inbound Leads need to be treated differently and be sold to differently. b) When done right, you can accelerate deal cycles and increase Win Rates. c) It gives us data on the exact objections coming up in the Sales Process, so we can -- once again -- address it earlier in our Marketing Messaging.

4. We launch 1 - 3 experiments every Week

Every week, we sit together and launch at-least ONE experiment that we expect will positively improve a core metric. This could be Landing Page Conversion, Homepage Activation, or even the Meeting Booking Rate. 

If there is a metric that is lagging, for example, Meeting Show up Rates, then we'll launch an experiment to fix that too. Every single KPI is up for improvement with a controlled experiment.

Why? This compounds. You make a 1% to even 5% improvement on a single KPI, the entire flywheel starts to move faster.

5. Everything is tracked on ONE spreadsheet with a week representing each Column

Every team member has access to this. All our AI Agents have access to this. And we all stare at it weekly during our War Room Meetings and we see how things are trending Week Over Week for each of our KPIs. 

Sales is not an enemy 
But they can be if you don't align end-to-end as Marketing.
