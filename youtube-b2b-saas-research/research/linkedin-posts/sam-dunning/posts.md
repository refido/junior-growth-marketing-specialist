I made 4 founders doing nr $7M+ ARR combined share their best b2b marketing advice for 2026 over a steak dinner:

1. Think in ecocsystems. ICPs might find you recommended in AI search, flag you up with their team, check your founders LinkedIn, watch some YouTube, forget about it, then entually come back when they're ready."how did you hear about us" form might say linkedin. But on the sales call they could say: ai search, then youtube, then linkedin etc.

2. Be radically transparent in social content. Share full playbooks. What worked, what didn't, the highs, the lows, the marketing experiment that flopped like crazy and the one that worked super well. You'll repel people you don't want to work with and attract the ones who think "this person actually knows what they're talking about."

3. Trust is at an all-time low. The way to fight it is to focus on content formats that build the most trust and are the hardest to replicate. Think long-form. Newsletters, longer blog posts, YouTube, podcasts. Short posts catch people at the top. Long content gets them at the end where they actually buy.

4. Get on camera. LinkedIn feeds are full of slop. When it comes to video there's a much bigger barrier to entry. People see your face, hear your voice, watch you talk for 10 to 20 minutes about a specific topic. Very hard to replicate. That's exactly why you should do it.

5. If you run ads, get very specific. Split your customers into many different buckets because the likelihood is they face very specific problems. The more specific your ad creative, the better Meta can deliver it and the higher your conversion rate. Then make sure where you're sending them mentions that specific problem too. Don't send people to the homepage. Build a page that is specific to that audience.

6. Figure out your 1 or 2 best channels and go all in. Look at your pipeline and your sales CRM. How have you brought in your best, most profitable, most enjoyable to work with clients? It's probably only 1, 2, or 3 channels. Stop messing around with the other things and 10x down on those. Exhaust it. Then worry about expanding into new channels.

7. Build your personal brand now, and then get your whole team doing it. If you have 3 founders, all 3 should be posting. You get 3 times the reach. Then get your managers posting. Then your whole team. Two years from now you'll look back and be glad you started. Anyone can put up an AI-written post on LinkedIn. People have an AI radar right now. They can see it. Video is where you can still stand out.

8. Be consistent with your distribution e.g. B2B teams turn SEO on and off depending on how it performs a couple of months in, thinking it works like paid ads where you just flip a switch. SEO has to be done consistently over months and years to build up and compound. And here's what most people miss: if you're ever planning to exit your company, organic distribution can add to valuation.
